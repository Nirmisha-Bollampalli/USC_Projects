needed for the Fourth Annual Student Biotech Expo www studentbiotech org The Student Biotech Expo is hosted by the Washington Biotechnology Foundation WBF a non profit organization dedicated to increasing the understanding of biotechnology through education The Expo will be held March th at Meydenbauer Center Bellevue The Student Biotech Expo draws together high school students from thirteen Seattle area biology biotechnology and molecular biology classes and allows them to present their work to each other and to the public The Expo promotes science literacy and allows students to showcase work that reflects different aspects of biotechnology and biomedicine Two key elements of the Expo distinguish it from conventional science fairs First each student is matched with a community mentor who has experience in the student s area of interest This program facilitates connections between the scientific and education communities and provide increased opportunities for student access to resources Second students projects can be presented in a variety of forms from art drama or music to molecular modeling web design or traditional scientific research For all projects students must research the science behind their chosen topics Students have the opportunity to see the relevance of science to their own interests and can demonstrate their learning in ways that best suit them Mentors are invaluable assets to students The role of the mentor is to serve as a reference to the student for their project Time Commitment varies from or more hours depending on type of contact We work with mentors to accommodate their availability Mentorships can range from email contact to internships Mentors who sign up within the next few weeks will have the opportunity to attend one of the mentor student get together at a host research facility Information about Mentorship including Sign Up Forms can be found at http www studentbiotech org mentor_info htm or contact Abby
