Associated Press OTTAWA Cleaning Polluted Urban Lands Could Bring Billion Benefit Crumbling warehouses rusting refineries and abandoned gas stations represent a major economic opportunity for Canada says a federal study Cleaning up an estimated so called brownfields in cities and towns could bring billion in benefits the National Round Table on the Environment and the Economy says in a report released Monday For ex le restoring brownfields in Toronto would probably bring an extra million in annual tax revenue said Angus Ross one of the study s authors The report does not estimate costs of cleaning up the thousands of polluted sites A brownfield is an abandoned vacant or derelict site contaminated by past industrial activity Loans grants and regulatory changes are needed to encourage remediation of such sites across Canada says the report Brownfields damage a neighborhoods image and quality of life and represent a major loss of economic opportunity says the report Restoring them would create jobs increase tax revenues reduce commuting times and ease pressure to develop cropland wooded areas and recreational land around cities Brownfield redevelopment in Canada has the
