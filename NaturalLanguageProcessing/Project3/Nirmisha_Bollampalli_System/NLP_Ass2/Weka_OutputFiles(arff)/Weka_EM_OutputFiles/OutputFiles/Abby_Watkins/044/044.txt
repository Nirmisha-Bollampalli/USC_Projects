at Arctic Coring Expedition Seeks Record of Earth Climate Scientists believe a million year record of the Earth s climate lies in an underwater mountain chain in the ice clogged waters near the North Pole An international team is drilling deep into the Arctic Ocean ridge for the first time in a technically complex effort to extract sediment that will provide a climatic history and may help explain how humans are changing the planet Think of it as a book said Kate Moran an associate professor in the ocean engineering department at the University of Rhode Island and a co chair of the Arctic Coring Expedition We re turning back the pages of time Scientists have wanted to make this trip for decades but have never had the combination of icebreaking ships and advanced drilling equipment used by the oil industry to pull it off ARCHAEOLOGISTS SEARCH FOR SLAVE SHIP IN CARIBBEAN Archaeologists are leading an expedition in hopes of finding a Spanish ship that wrecked along the jagged reefs off the Turks and Caicos Islands in carrying a cargo of African slaves The story of the Trouvadore is unusual because all slaves made it to shore and all but one survived to see their freedom granted by a British government that had just outlawed slavery Most settled in the arid low lying islands and began new lives working its salt ponds and raising families The shipwreck holds particular significance for the British territory of people because researchers believe virtually all native islanders have ties by blood or marriage to the survivors EXPEDITION NOTES North Face Climbers Say Eye Care x The Edmund Hillary s oxygen canister and Dr Livingstone s hat They are among artifacts available to the public for the first time from the archives of the Royal Geographical Society the year old British institution that sent explorers to the South Pole up Mount Everest and in search of the source of the Nile and amassed one of the world s largest geographical archives The London based society has opened an airy extension to its Victorian headquarters housing some two million records maps charts books and photographs Society director Rita Gardner said that the six year million project had moved the documents from Dickensian conditions in the building s basement to climate controlled rooms in the new wing EXPEDITION MARKETING Arctic Team to Carry New Emergency Beacon x One reason companies Lonnie Dupre of Minnesota will serve as a proving ground for the new TerraFix GPS Personal Locator Beacon PLB from ACR Electronics Ft Lauderdale Fla The TerraFix is a satellite detectable beacon that
